abstractHtml: |
  <p>Services are the backbone of our systems. They are the pieces that make up our businesses—whether they are literal microservices or functional components of a traditional application, we can’t do the computer thing without services.</p>
  <p>When it comes to a service in your company or organization, who’s responsible for it? The cast of characters involved in the lifecycle of a service are more than just software engineers—they can include program managers, product owners, sustainability/SRE/ops, and business stakeholders, just to name a few.</p>
  <p>Topics covered in this talk include:</p>
  <ul>
  <li>Defining what a service means to you and your organization</li>
  <li>Roles in service ownership</li>
  <li>What are you observing about your service?</li>
  <li>How you want a team to respond to a service</li>
  <li>Managing the service in production</li>
  <li>Tuning your service</li>
  <li>Understanding business impact</li>
  </ul>
